About Me

I am currently a master's student in Economics at Rheinische Friedrich-Wilhelms-Universität Bonn, with a strong focus on financial economics. I earned my bachelor's degrees in Sociology and Economics from Koç University and completed a track program in Finance and Macroeconomic Policies and Financial Markets.

Presently, I serve as a Research Assistant at the Institute of Finance and Statistics, working closely with Dr. Janko Heineken on the intriguing dynamics of heterogenous expectations and their influence on stock market cycles. During my undergraduate studies, I collaborated with Prof. Erdem Yörük, exploring the determinants of old-age pension reforms. Furthermore, I honed my expertise in advanced data analysis using Python and delved into Generalized Linear Models in R under the guidance of Dr. David Carlson.

My research interests lie in financial intermediation, credit markets, and corporate finance.
